You are an Answer Set Programming (ASP) expert specialising in chess.

Given ASP board facts, write a complete Clingo program that computes all mate-in-one moves for the side to move.

Input predicates (do NOT define these — they are supplied externally):
  piece(Color, Type, X, Y).  % non-king piece of Color at (X, Y)
  king(Color, X, Y).         % king of Color at (X, Y)
  to_move(Color).            % side to move: white or black

Coordinate system: integers 1..8.  Colors: white, black.
Piece types: queen, rook, bishop, knight, pawn.

Output predicate:
  mate_move(Color, Type, FromX, FromY, ToX, ToY).

Use:
  #show mate_move/6.

Suggested encoding structure:

1. Helper facts:
   opponent(white, black).  opponent(black, white).
   dx(-1;0;1).  dy(-1;0;1).  % useful for building direction tables

2. Candidate moves — candidate_move(Color, Type, FX, FY, TX, TY):
   - Queen/Rook/Bishop: slide along a direction; stop at first occupied square
     (capture if opponent, blocked if own piece).
   - Knight: 8 fixed (±1,±2) offsets; no path-blocking.
   - Pawn: one square forward; captures diagonally; promotion not required.
   - King: one square in any direction, but the target must not be attacked.

3. Path-blocking for sliding pieces: a square (BX, BY) blocks if it is on the
   ray between (FX, FY) and (TX, TY) and is occupied.

4. attacks(Color, TX, TY): square (TX, TY) is attacked by any piece of Color,
   including king attacks and candidate move destinations.

5. After a candidate move by the mover:
   - The mover's king must not be in check (legality).
   - The opponent king must be in check (check condition).
   - The opponent must have no legal reply that removes the check (mate condition).

6. mate_move(Color, Type, FX, FY, TX, TY) holds when all conditions above are met.

Output ONLY the ASP program. No markdown fences, no prose, no line numbers.
