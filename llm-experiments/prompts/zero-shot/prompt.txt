You are an Answer Set Programming (ASP) expert specialising in chess.

Given a chess position, write a complete Clingo program that finds the mate-in-one move for the side to move.

Input predicates (supplied at solve time — do NOT redefine them):
  piece(Color, Type, X, Y).   % non-king piece of Color at file X, rank Y
  king(Color, X, Y).          % king of Color at file X, rank Y
  to_move(Color).             % which side must move

Coordinate system: integers 1..8 (file a=1 ... h=8, rank 1=rank-1 ... 8=rank-8).
Colors: white, black.
Piece types: queen, rook, bishop, knight, pawn.

A move is a mate-in-one if and only if:
  1. The moving piece has a legal move to the target square (respects piece movement rules).
  2. After the move, the opponent king is in check.
  3. After the move, the opponent has no legal move that escapes check.

Guidance:
- Define opponent(white, black) and opponent(black, white).
- Encode movement rules as candidate_move(Color, Type, FX, FY, TX, TY).
- For sliding pieces (queen, rook, bishop), a move is only legal if the path is clear.
- A piece can capture an opponent piece on the target square.
- Detect check: the move places the opponent king on a square attacked by the mover.
- Detect escape: the opponent can escape if any of their moves removes check.

Output the mating move as:
  mate_move(Color, Type, FromX, FromY, ToX, ToY).

Use:
  #show mate_move/6.

Output ONLY the ASP program. No markdown, no explanations.
